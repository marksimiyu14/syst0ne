Library rador

Our library Rador can be intergrated with koha which makes it easier to issue/return books without necessarly interacting with koha.
RFID Tags: Books and other library materials are equipped with RFID tags. These tags contain information about the item.
RFID Readers: RFID readers are placed at strategic locations within the library, such as at the entrance/exit gates and near self-checkout kiosks.
Check-out/Disarm: When a patron wants to check out a book, they place it near the RFID reader. The reader scans the RFID tag, records the transaction, and updates the library's database.
Check-in/Arm: When a patron returns a book, the RFID tag is again scanned by a reader, and the library's database is updated accordingly.
Inventory Management: RFID technology enables librarians to conduct quick and accurate inventory checks by scanning all the books on a shelf simultaneously.
RFID Security Gates: RFID can also be used for security purposes. If a book hasn't been properly checked out, the RFID system can trigger an alarm at the exit gate.
